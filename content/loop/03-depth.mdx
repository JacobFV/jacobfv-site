# Depth

The third condition is the one I find hardest to summarize without
giving up too much.

Epistemic depth is not "the system has a self-model." Lots of systems
have self-models. The thermostat has a self-model in some
impoverished sense; so does GPT.

Epistemic depth is the property that the *world model contains the
knowledge that it exists*. It is the recursive coupling of the model
to itself, distributed across the hierarchy, continuously
self-evidencing.

</Scene>

<Scene>

## Field-evidencing

The authors call this *field-evidencing*. The world model knows
itself non-locally — meaning the knowing is not located in any one
node of the inference hierarchy; it is the aggregate dynamic of the
hierarchy as a whole.

This is the move that distinguishes *consciousness of* from *meta
consciousness about*. You can have the latter without the former. The
former, on their view, is constitutive.

</Scene>
