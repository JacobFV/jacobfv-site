The original framing: combine GLOM-style unsupervised neural networks in a
multi-agent setting where each agent has its own Linux VM and restricted
internet access; cycle the environment through periods of information
surplus and scarcity to do artificial selection on skill diversity.

The framing was too big to attack directly, but it was the seed for
several follow-ups: Computatrum, Multiparadigm Networks, the Multi-Agent
Network. Each of those carved off a piece that was actually buildable.

I'm leaving this here as the canonical reference for "what was I trying
to do in 2019," because the answer still informs the current work.
