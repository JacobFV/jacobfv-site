## Premise

Classical RL inherits a particular philosophical commitment from its origin
in operant conditioning: that goal-directed behavior reduces to maximizing
a scalar signal. This commitment is convenient mathematically and false
empirically.

The note collects the alternatives — multi-objective formulations,
preference-based learning, intrinsic motivation, free-energy minimization —
and asks what taking them seriously would change about how we structure
agents.

The honest answer is "almost everything, in ways we haven't built tooling
for yet."
