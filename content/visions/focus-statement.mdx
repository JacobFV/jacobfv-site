> The only constant is change. — Heraclitus, as quoted by Dr. Ron Cross.

We're in the middle of the steepest technoevolutionary leap in the
observable universe and most of it is locked behind cost. The single most
useful thing I can build is the artifact that pushes the cost-floor of
embodied general-purpose intelligence under the median household budget.

The work decomposes into:

1. A vertically integrated hydraulic/mechatronic stack with a manufacturing
   path I can self-finance through prototype.
2. A control / world-model stack that's teachable by demonstration and
   robust under deployment.
3. An economic model — on-demand swarm — that maximizes per-robot utility.

The longer essay version of this lives in the `/visions` route once the
3D scene lands in Phase 7. For now this MDX is the scaffold.
