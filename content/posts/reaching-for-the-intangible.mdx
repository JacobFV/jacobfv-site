We talk about understanding as if it were a property an artifact either has
or doesn't, but the word collapses under inspection. A thermostat understands
temperature in some impoverished sense. A child understands gravity well
enough not to fall down stairs. GPT understands grammar better than most
humans understand grammar.

The interesting question is not *whether* a system understands but *what
gradient* it is climbing. Understanding is an arrow, not a state.

## Three sketches

1. Understanding-as-compression. The system that represents the world more
   compactly understands it better. Trivially true and not very useful.
2. Understanding-as-prediction. Stronger; ties to free-energy minimization.
   Still misses the *for what* — prediction toward what end?
3. Understanding-as-action. The system understands what it can act on
   skillfully. This one is closer to what I mean when I say a child
   understands gravity.

I keep returning to the third one and I keep distrusting it.
